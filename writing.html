About the book

Before Google Era
Dehradun, India

You are never more alive than as a teenager. With numerous bodily changes and with the brain flush with chemicals, teenage is a time of great tumult, especially for young men in an all-boys boarding school.

Jai and his friends provide enough opportunities to hark back to those days of being younger. They remind us of when we acted saucy with our teacher or for that matter benefitted from a father figure in the form of another teacher. They take us down memory to feel how it was to have a crush on the girl in the school across the road or fancied a teacher in school.

In their stories is the exhilaration of bunking school, the grit of fighting it out on the playing field, and of course, the heady feeling of falling in love. The pressure of examinations, the thrill of adventure, and matters of honour are integral to their stories.

Will they come good and will there always be a happy ending?

Writing

Manavendra was never a writer; it was his younger sister who would write articles and poems in the local newspapers during her school days. However, he grew up seeing his father's opinion pieces being published often in newspapers. He used to be part of his school's literary society and had contributed to the school newsletter occasionally. It was on the encouragement of his economics professor during his post-graduate studies did he manage his first publication. Since then he has written numerous times across a wide variety of newspapers and other periodicals.
