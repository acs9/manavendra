Hi...

Manavendra wasn't a natural writer; it was his younger sister who would write articles and poems in the newspapers during her school days. However he grew up seeing his father's opinion pieces being published often in newspapers. He used to be part of his school's literary society and had contributed to the school newsletter occassionally. It was on the encouragement of his economics professor during his post graduate studies did he manage his first publication. Since then he has written numerous times across a wide variety of newspapers and other periodicals.

Quick Bio

  • Education:

Manavendra Prasad did his schooling from a boarding Welham Boys' School, a boarding schhol in Dehradun. He graduated from Ramjas College, University of Delhi, BA (Honours) Economics, and thereafter did MBA from Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies, Mumbai.
His debut book entitled "Those were the Days" is a collection of short stories about young boys growing up in a boarding school – an easy to read commercial fiction.

About the Book 

Interests

  • Financial Services
    Manavendra has spent considerable time working his way up the corporate ladder. He started his career with a mutual fund company and later worked in the wealth management space and in retail banking in large Indian Banks. His abiding interest in financial services continues.
  • Politics & International Relations
    Politics and International relations gets Manavendra excited. He closely tracks and follows various narrative in the world of politics. To improve his understanding of IR he completed a certificate program in International Relations.
  • History
    Manavendra is a history buff. He likes to visit places associated with India's history. He loves reading about history, William Dalrymple's writings are amongst the favourite.
  • Bihar
    Anything to do with Bihar is close to Manavendra's heart. From fending off Laloo jokes in early years of his career to the cuisine, the fruits and of course hsitory Manavendra loves anything Bihari.
